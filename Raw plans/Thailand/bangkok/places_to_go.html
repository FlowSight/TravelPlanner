Iconic Temples

Temple Highlights Entry Fee Notes
Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) Thailand's most sacred Buddha image, golden spires, intricate mosaics ~500 THB (includes Grand Palace) Modest dress required
Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha) 46m gold-plated Reclining Buddha, oldest & largest temple ~200 THB Thai massage school on site
Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn) Colorful porcelain spire, river views, climbable prang ~100 THB Best at sunset

Historical & Cultural Sites

Place Highlights Entry Fee Tips
The Grand Palace Former royal residence, elaborate Thai architecture Included with Wat Phra Kaew Visit early morning
Jim Thompson House Thai teak houses, silk & Asian art museum ~200 THB Named after silk industry reviver

Getting Around

Transport Type Best For Notes
BTS Skytrain Elevated rail Central areas Fast & air-conditioned
MRT Underground metro Connects to BTS Reliable
Grab Rideshare app Door-to-door Better than taxis
Tuk-tuks 3-wheeled vehicle Short trips, fun Negotiate price first
Chao Phraya Express Boat River transport Scenic travel Cheap & avoids traffic

Tips

Topic Tip
Best time to visit November to February (cool & dry)
Currency Thai Baht (THB) - ~35 THB = 1 USD
Temple etiquette Remove shoes, dress modestly
Bargaining Expected at markets, not in malls
Hydration Carry water - it gets very hot!
Convenience 7-Eleven everywhere for cheap snacks & essentials
